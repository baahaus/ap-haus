Saturdays at Bloom's — going on thirty-plus years

CIGAR CAMP

Camp in session — pull up a chairSaturday from noon

— It started as a bookkeeping day.

Every Saturday a few of Mark's friends would come keep him company while he did the books: smoke a few cigars, shoot the bull. The visitors kept multiplying until the bookkeeping quietly became the side show. By the mid-90s a local paper was already calling Bloom's the pioneer of the whole sit-and-smoke shop idea, "later copied by many others."

"It was a bookkeeping day for me." Mark — on how Cigar Camp began
