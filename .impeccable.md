# Design Context — ap.haus

## Target audience
Owner-operators of trades and local service businesses in Pittsburgh and the
Alle-Kiski Valley: contractors, restoration firms, lawn/landscape services,
sports facilities, small shops. $500K–$20M revenue, currently on a dated
Divi/Wix site or no site at all. They buy people, not agencies. Secondary
audience: technical referrers who knew ap.haus as a research/tools site
(served by /lab, not the main site).

## Use cases
- See proof fast: before/after of real local sites, prices, one clear CTA.
- Request the free homepage rebuild (the teardown lead magnet).
- Book a 15-minute call / send a structured quote request.
- Verify Brandon is real and credible (face, name, day job at Graciano).

## Brand personality / tone
Editorial studio restraint at reference grade (Brandon's picked refs: CTHDRL's
hairline grid + mono metadata, Century's billboard hero scale, Exo Ape's
oversized type over real imagery) — executed LIGHT. Warm near-white ground,
near-black ink, ONE oxide-red accent used sparingly (headline phrase, CTA
hover, small marks). Sentence case, not shouty uppercase. Bricolage Grotesque
display, Instrument Sans body, IBM Plex Mono metadata, Silkscreen wordmark
only. Confident, plainspoken, first-person singular; solo is a feature.
Never: dark mode (confirmed 2026-07-06 even though his refs were dark), theme
cosplay (v2's estimate-packet stamps/highlighter read amateur — rejected),
generic serif studio templates (v1 — rejected), fake before/afters, agency
plural-"we", jargon ("AI agents" → "office automation").

## Voice rules
- Flat fees shown openly; never hourly rates.
- Only real claims: no invented testimonials, metrics, or client counts.
- Graciano may be named openly as employer and demo subject.
- DeSanto's is "launching soon" until the site is live for the business.
